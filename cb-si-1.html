Furman Center Neighborhood Profile

St. George/Stapleton - Community Board Profile

View full profile ↗

Summary

In 2023, St. George/Stapleton was the city’s 18th largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the 33rd largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 32nd highest median income, and the 41st most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 1,568 new housing units, 474 units of which were market rate and 1,094 units of which were income restricted.

  • In 2023, there were an estimated 167,916 people in St. George/Stapleton, of which 11.8% identified as Asian, 22.5% identified as Black, 31.4% identified as Hispanic, and 29.8% identified as White.
  • In 2023, the household income group with the largest share (36.0%) of households was $100,001 – $250,000. In 2000, the same household income group had the largest share as well, with a slightly lower share of 34.9%.
  • Median household income in 2023 was $75,600, about 5% less than citywide median household income ($79,480). The poverty rate in St. George/Stapleton was 23.7% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide.
  • Real median gross rent in St. George/Stapleton increased from $1,440 in 2006 to $1,580 in 2023. This represents a 9.7% increase over the same period. The overall rental vacancy rate in St. George/Stapleton was 6.2% in 2023.
  • As of 2023, the change in median gross rent outpaced the change in median household income by 19.3 percentage points. In 2023, 31.0% of renter households in St. George/Stapleton were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent). 82.6% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 18 percentage points higher than the share in 2010.
  • In 2023, the homeownership rate in St. George/Stapleton was 52.8%, which is higher than the citywide share of 32.5%.
  • The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has decreased by 5.5 percentage points since 2010.
  • In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 22.3 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 5.6 per 1,000 properties in the neighborhood. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in St. George/Stapleton, 5.2% and 5.2% were high cost loans, respectively. 267 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in St. George/Stapleton in 2022. There were 7.2 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.
