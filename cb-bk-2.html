Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights (BK02) - Brooklyn Profile

View full profile ↗

Summary

In 2023, Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights was the city’s 41st largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the 43rd largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 5th highest median income, and the 5th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 19,770 new housing units, 15,141 units of which were market rate and 4,629 units of which were income restricted.

  • In 2023, Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights was the city’s 41st largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods.
  • Median household income in 2023 was $156,870, about 97% more than citywide median household income ($79,480)
  • The poverty rate in Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights was 10.8% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide
  • Real median gross rent in Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights increased from $1,420 in 2006 to $3,080 in 2023. This represents a 116.9% increase over the same period
  • The overall rental vacancy rate in Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights was 4.0% in 2023
  • As of 2023, the change in median household income outpaced the change in median gross rent by 16.6 percentage points
  • In 2023, 19.9% of renter households in Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights were severely rent burdened
  • 15.2% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 11 percentage points lower than the share in 2010
  • In 2023, the homeownership rate in Fort Greene/Brooklyn Heights was 29.9%, which is lower than the citywide share of 32.5%
  • The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has decreased by 1.2 percentage points since 2010
  • The serious crime rate, including property and violent crime types, was 21.2 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, compared to 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide
