Summary

In 2023, South Ozone Park/Howard Beach was the city’s 47th largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the 19th largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 19th highest median income, and the 15th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 15 new housing units, 12 units of which were market rate and 3 units of which were income restricted. Median household income in 2023 was $92,520, about 16% more than citywide median household income ($79,480). The poverty rate in South Ozone Park/Howard Beach was 11.4% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide. Real median gross rent in South Ozone Park/Howard Beach increased from $1,760 in 2006 to $2,100 in 2023. In 2023, the homeownership rate in South Ozone Park/Howard Beach was 67.8%, which is higher than the citywide share of 32.5%. The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has increased by 2.6 percentage points since 2010. In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 17.8 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 5.2 per 1,000 properties in the neighborhood. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in South Ozone Park/Howard Beach, 4.5% and 7.5% were high cost loans, respectively. 158 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in South Ozone Park/Howard Beach in 2022. There were 6.6 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.
