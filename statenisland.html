Furman Center — Staten Island Borough Profile

View full profile ↗
Economy

Staten Island has the 2nd highest median income of any borough in New York City, and it is the most homeowner-dominated borough in the city. Tottenville and Great Kills (SI3) ranks 10th citywide for median income. South Beach and Willowbrook (SI2) ranks 15th. St. George and Stapleton (SI1) ranks 32nd — meaningfully lower than the rest of the borough, reflecting the more economically mixed character of the North Shore versus the predominantly higher-income South Shore.

The within-borough contrast in Staten Island runs along a geographic and demographic divide that has defined the island for decades. The South Shore is wealthier, whiter, more suburban in character. The North Shore — St. George, Stapleton, Port Richmond, New Brighton — is lower-income, more racially diverse, and has historically been underserved by investment and infrastructure. SI1 ranking 32nd while SI3 ranks 10th is not a small gap. For a borough with only three community boards, it's a significant split.

Rent & Burden

Staten Island ranks 4th among the five boroughs for rent expense, but the dynamics are different from Manhattan or Brooklyn. The island is more owner-occupied, so the rental market is smaller and less analyzed. Rents on the North Shore are lower in absolute terms than in many Brooklyn or Queens districts, but rent burden is still real in SI1 — incomes on the North Shore don't always translate to capacity to absorb rising rents. The island's relative economic strength as a borough can obscure pockets of genuine hardship concentrated in the North Shore CDs.

Housing Production

Staten Island added the fewest new housing units of any borough between 2010 and 2024 — a function of geography, zoning, and political culture that has long resisted density. The island has limited transit, limited developable land in many areas, and a homeownership culture that is skeptical of multifamily production. That calculus is beginning to shift, particularly on the North Shore where rezoning and transit investment discussions have been active, but production remains constrained relative to need.

Demographics

Staten Island has the 5th largest borough population and the smallest nonwhite share of any borough. It is the least dense and most car-dependent borough in the city. The North Shore is the exception to several of those generalizations — it is denser, more transit-accessible, and more racially diverse than the rest of the island. The three community boards capture that divide to some extent, but a three-board structure covering 58 square miles and 500,000 people leaves each board managing an enormous geographic and demographic range.
