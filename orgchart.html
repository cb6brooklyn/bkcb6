The People of the City of New York

Citywide Elected

Mayor of New York City

Chief executive of city government. Appoints agency commissioners, Deputy Mayors, and most top officials. Proposes the city budget. Currently: Mayor Zohran Mamdani (took office Jan 1, 2026).

Citywide Elected

City Comptroller

Independent financial watchdog. Audits city agencies, manages pension funds, reviews contracts. Currently publishes the NYC Organization Chart used as a source here.

Citywide Elected

Public Advocate

Ombudsman for New Yorkers. Investigates complaints against city agencies, proposes legislation, and serves as first in line of mayoral succession.

Legislative Branch

NYC City Council — 51 Members

The legislative body. Passes local laws, adopts the city budget, and has final say on land use through ULURP. Bronx: 8 · Brooklyn: 16 · Manhattan: 10 · Queens: 14 · Staten Island: 3. The City Council's practice of member deference — allowing individual members to effectively veto development in their districts — was a central issue in the 2025 charter ballot measures.

Borough Elected

Borough Presidents (5)

One per borough. Advisory role in land use, appoint community board members, advocate for borough needs. CB6 members are appointed by the Brooklyn BP with City Council input.

Borough Elected

District Attorneys (5)

One per borough. Independently elected. Prosecute crimes in their county. Separate from the mayor's office — the mayor does not direct DA offices.

Local Advisory

Community Boards (59 citywide · CB6 serves Park Slope, Gowanus, Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Red Hook & more)

Each of NYC's 59 community districts has a board of up to 50 volunteer members. CBs have advisory — not binding — authority on land use, city budgets, and local services. Since the 2018 charter amendment, members are limited to four consecutive two-year terms. CB6 covers approximately 120,000 residents across 13,755+ tax lots.

First Deputy Mayor — Dean Fuleihan

▼

Advises the Mayor on citywide administrative, operational, and policy matters. Oversees the Office of Management and Budget, Department of Finance, Department of City Planning, Landmarks Preservation Commission, NYCHA, HPD, and related agencies.

Office of Management & Budget
Prepares the city's annual budget and tracks spending across all agencies. CB6 submits annual budget priorities to OMB.
Dept of Finance
Collects taxes, assesses property values, and processes payments. Property tax assessments affect every lot in CB6.
Dept of City Planning
Develops land use policy, manages zoning, and runs ULURP. Central to how neighborhoods grow and change.
Landmarks Preservation Commission
Designates and regulates historic landmarks and districts. CB6 has six historic districts and reviews CoA applications.
NYCHA
NYC Housing Authority. CB6 includes Gowanus Houses and Wyckoff Gardens. ~$200M in capital investment secured through the 2021 Gowanus rezoning.
HPD
Dept of Housing Preservation & Development. Finances and regulates affordable housing citywide.
Dept of Small Business Services
Supports small businesses with permits, workforce development, and commercial corridor programs.
Dept of Consumer & Worker Protection
Enforces consumer protection and labor standards laws, licenses businesses, and investigates complaints.
Dept of Cultural Affairs
Funds and supports NYC's museums, theaters, and cultural institutions.
Office to Protect Tenants
Coordinates city efforts to prevent tenant harassment, displacement, and illegal evictions.
Office of Media & Entertainment
Supports NYC's media and entertainment industries and issues film production permits.
NYC Tourism & Conventions
Promotes NYC as a travel destination and manages convention business.
Public Design Commission
Reviews proposed works of art, architecture, and landscape on city-owned property.
Office for Economic Opportunity
Coordinates anti-poverty programs and measures socioeconomic outcomes across agencies.
Office of M/WBE
Promotes and certifies Minority and Women-Owned Business Enterprises for city contracting.
Office of Talent & Workforce Development
Coordinates workforce training and career development programs across the city.

Deputy Mayor for Housing & Planning — Leila Bozorg

▼

Oversees housing production, land use planning, and community development. Most relevant to CB6's ULURP reviews, affordable housing work, and the Gowanus rezoning implementation.

HPD
Dept of Housing Preservation & Development. Finances and regulates affordable housing. Administers MIH and related programs.
HDC
Housing Development Corporation. Provides low-cost financing for affordable housing through bonds and other tools.
Dept of City Planning
Manages zoning and runs ULURP. CB6 interacts with DCP on every land use application in the district.
Office of Housing Recovery
Manages post-disaster housing recovery programs including Sandy rebuild initiatives.

Deputy Mayor for Economic Justice — Julie Su

▼

Oversees economic development, workforce, and business support agencies with a focus on equitable economic growth.

EDC
Economic Development Corporation. Leads major development projects including the Brooklyn Marine Terminal redevelopment.
Dept of Small Business Services
Supports small businesses, workforce development, and neighborhood commercial corridor programs.
Dept of Consumer & Worker Protection
Enforces consumer protection and labor standards, licenses businesses, investigates complaints.

Deputy Mayor for Health & Human Services — Helen Arteaga Landaverde

▼

Oversees the agencies that provide direct services to New Yorkers — healthcare, housing assistance, children's services, and homelessness.

HRA / Dept of Social Services
Human Resources Administration. Administers SNAP, Medicaid, cash assistance, and other safety net programs.
Dept of Homeless Services
Operates the city's shelter system and outreach programs for people experiencing homelessness.
Administration for Children's Services
Protects children from abuse and neglect, manages foster care and adoption.
NYC Health + Hospitals
NYC's public hospital system — 11 hospitals serving all New Yorkers regardless of ability to pay.

Deputy Mayor for Operations — Julia Kerson

▼

Oversees the agencies that keep the city running day to day — streets, sanitation, parks, buildings, environmental protection, and infrastructure.

Dept of Transportation
Manages city streets, bridges, traffic signals, and bike infrastructure. Relevant to CB6's street safety work and Sammy's Law implementation.
Dept of Sanitation
Collects trash and recycling, manages composting, and clears snow.
Dept of Parks & Recreation
Operates NYC's 30,000+ acres of parkland including Prospect Park — within CB6's service area.
Dept of Buildings
Issues permits, inspects construction, and enforces building code. CB6 tracks DOB activity via the dashboard's address lookup.
Dept of Environmental Protection
Manages water supply, wastewater, and the Gowanus Canal Superfund and Red Hook CSO facility.
Dept of Design & Construction
Manages capital construction projects for city agencies.
School Construction Authority
Plans and builds new school buildings and manages capital improvements to existing schools.
Taxi & Limousine Commission
Regulates yellow cabs, green cars, and app-based rideshare services.

Deputy Mayor for Community Safety — Renita Francois

▼

Oversees and coordinates the operations of the Department of Community Safety.

NYPD
NYC Police Department. CB6 is served by the 76th Precinct (Carroll Gardens/Red Hook) and the 78th Precinct (Park Slope/Gowanus).
