The People of the City of New York

Residents of the Bronx, Brooklyn, Manhattan, Queens & Staten Island

Citywide Elected

Mayor of New York City

Chief executive of city government. Appoints agency commissioners, Deputy Mayors, and most top officials. Proposes the city budget. Currently: Mayor Zohran Mamdani (took office Jan 1, 2026).

Citywide Elected

City Comptroller

Independent financial watchdog. Audits city agencies, manages pension funds, reviews contracts. Currently publishes the NYC Organization Chart used as a source here.

Citywide Elected

Public Advocate

Ombudsman for New Yorkers. Investigates complaints against city agencies, proposes legislation, and serves as first in line of mayoral succession.

Legislative Branch

NYC City Council — 51 Members

The legislative body. Passes local laws, adopts the city budget, and has final say on land use through ULURP. Bronx: 8 · Brooklyn: 16 · Manhattan: 10 · Queens: 14 · Staten Island: 3. The City Council's practice of member deference — allowing individual members to effectively veto development in their districts — was a central issue in the 2025 charter ballot measures.

Borough Elected

Borough Presidents (5)

One per borough. Advisory role in land use, appoint community board members, advocate for borough needs. CB6 members are appointed by the Brooklyn BP with City Council input.

Borough Elected

District Attorneys (5)

One per borough. Independently elected. Prosecute crimes in their county. Separate from the mayor's office — the mayor does not direct DA offices.

Local Advisory

Community Boards (59 citywide · CB6 serves Park Slope, Gowanus, Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Red Hook & more)

Each of NYC's 59 community districts has a board of up to 50 volunteer members. CBs have advisory — not binding — authority on land use, city budgets, and local services. Since the 2018 charter amendment, members are limited to four consecutive two-year terms. CB6 covers approximately 120,000 residents across 13,755+ tax lots.

Executive Branch

Office of the Mayor

The Mayor appoints 8 Deputy Mayors, each overseeing a portfolio of agencies. Tap any Deputy Mayor below to see their agencies.

Deputy Mayor for Housing, Economic Development & Workforce

▼

Oversees the agencies responsible for housing production, economic growth, job creation, and cultural life. Most relevant to CB6's land use work and the 2025 charter changes.

HPD
Dept of Housing Preservation & Development. Finances and regulates affordable housing. Administers the new ELURP-CC and BSA Fast-track processes.
HDC
Housing Development Corporation. Provides low-cost financing for affordable housing projects through bonds and other tools.
Dept of City Planning
Develops land use policy, manages zoning, and runs ULURP. Central to how neighborhoods grow and change.
EDC
Economic Development Corporation. Promotes business growth, manages city-owned assets, and leads major development projects including the Brooklyn Marine Terminal.
Dept of Small Business Services
Supports small businesses with permits, workforce development, and neighborhood commercial corridor programs.
Dept of Consumer & Worker Protection
Enforces consumer protection and labor standards laws, licenses businesses, and investigates complaints.
Landmarks Preservation Commission
Designates and regulates historic landmarks and districts. CB6 has raised concerns about LPC's role in housing unit consolidation — CB6 has lost ~1,500 units since 2010 in landmarked districts.
Dept of Cultural Affairs
Funds and supports NYC's museums, theaters, and cultural institutions.
NYCHA
NYC Housing Authority. Manages public housing for over 400,000 residents. Federally funded and overseen, but city-administered.
Office to Protect Tenants
Coordinates city efforts to prevent tenant harassment, displacement, and illegal evictions.
Office of Nightlife
Advocates for the nightlife industry and mediates between venues, residents, and agencies.
NYC Tourism & Conventions
Promotes NYC as a travel destination and manages convention business.

Deputy Mayor for Operations

▼

Manages the agencies that keep the city running day to day — streets, sanitation, parks, buildings, and infrastructure.

Dept of Transportation
Manages city streets, bridges, traffic signals, and bike infrastructure. Approved the Court Street bike lane in CB6.
Dept of Sanitation
Collects trash and recycling, manages composting, and clears snow. One of the largest agencies by workforce.
Dept of Parks & Recreation
Operates NYC's 30,000+ acres of parkland including Prospect Park and the Parade Ground — both within CB6's service area.
Dept of Buildings
Issues permits, inspects construction, and enforces building code. CB6 tracks DOB activity for address lookups in the dashboard.
Dept of Environmental Protection
Manages water supply, wastewater, and the Gowanus Canal Superfund and Red Hook CSO facility — both major CB6 infrastructure projects.
Dept of Design & Construction
Manages capital construction projects for city agencies including new buildings, water mains, and sewers.
Taxi & Limousine Commission
Regulates yellow cabs, green cars, and app-based rideshare services.
NYC 311
The city's non-emergency service request and information line. CB6 tracks 311 complaint data by neighborhood and category.
Office of Management & Budget
Prepares the city's annual budget and tracks spending across all agencies.
School Construction Authority
Plans and builds new school buildings and manages capital improvements to existing schools.

Deputy Mayor for Health & Human Services

▼

Oversees the agencies that provide direct services to New Yorkers — healthcare, housing assistance, aging, children, and immigration support.

Dept of Health & Mental Hygiene
Public health agency. Tracks disease, sets health policy, runs community health programs, and oversees mental health services.
Health + Hospitals
NYC's public hospital system — 11 hospitals serving all New Yorkers regardless of ability to pay.
Dept of Social Services / HRA
Human Resources Administration. Administers SNAP, Medicaid, cash assistance, and other safety net programs.
Dept of Homeless Services
Operates the city's shelter system and outreach programs for people experiencing homelessness.
Administration for Children's Services
Protects children from abuse and neglect, manages foster care and adoption.
Dept for the Aging
Funds senior centers, home care, and other services for older New Yorkers.
Office of Immigrant Affairs
Connects immigrants to city services and advocates for immigrant communities.
Office to End Domestic Violence
Coordinates city response to domestic and gender-based violence, operates shelters and legal services.
Office of the Chief Medical Examiner
Investigates deaths, performs autopsies, and maintains death records.
Dept of Veterans' Services
Connects veterans and their families to benefits, housing, and employment services.

Deputy Mayor for Public Safety

▼

Oversees law enforcement, emergency services, corrections, and criminal justice coordination.

NYPD
NYC Police Department. CB6 is served by the 76th Precinct (Carroll Gardens/Red Hook), 78th Precinct (Park Slope), and 84th Precinct (parts of Boerum Hill).
Fire Department (FDNY)
Fire suppression, emergency medical services, and fire prevention.
Dept of Correction
Manages city jails including Rikers Island and borough-based facilities.
NYC Emergency Management
Coordinates the city's response to emergencies and disasters — weather, public health, infrastructure failures.
Dept of Probation
Supervises people on probation and connects them to services and employment.
Office of Criminal Justice
Coordinates criminal justice policy across agencies and manages alternative-to-incarceration programs.
Civilian Complaint Review Board
Independent board that investigates complaints of NYPD misconduct.
NYC Cyber Command
Defends city systems against cyber threats and coordinates cybersecurity across agencies.

Deputy Mayor for Strategic Initiatives

▼

Coordinates cross-agency priorities including education, technology, climate, and citywide planning.

Dept of Education
Largest school system in the US — over 1 million students, 1,800 schools. Budget set by the mayor and city council.
City University of New York (CUNY)
Public university system of 25 colleges including Brooklyn College, which is in CB6's service area.
Office of Technology & Innovation
Leads citywide digital services, broadband equity, and technology modernization.
Office of Climate & Environmental Justice
Coordinates the city's climate policy and environmental justice work across agencies.
Civic Engagement Commission
Created by the 2018 charter amendment. Runs participatory budgeting and civic outreach programs citywide.
Office of Pensions & Investments
Manages the city's five public employee pension funds.
NYC Service
Coordinates volunteer programs and connects New Yorkers to service opportunities.
Office of Food Policy
Coordinates food access programs and nutrition policy across city agencies.

Deputy Mayor for Administration & Chief of Staff

▼

Manages the internal operations of city government — contracting, personnel, finance, and compliance.

Dept of Citywide Administrative Services
Manages city real estate, fleet, procurement, and HR services for city employees.
Dept of Finance
Collects taxes, assesses property values, and processes payments. Property tax assessments affect every lot in CB6.
Dept of Investigation
Independent watchdog that investigates corruption and misconduct in city government.
Law Department
City's legal counsel. Represents the city in litigation and provides legal opinions to agencies.
Office of Labor Relations
Negotiates and administers contracts with the city's municipal labor unions.
Conflicts of Interest Board
Enforces ethics rules for city employees and elected officials.
Office of Contract Services
Oversees procurement and contracting across city agencies.
Office of Payroll Administration
Manages payroll for the city's 300,000+ employees.

Deputy Mayor for Communications

▼

Manages the city's public communications, press operations, and media channels.

Press Office
Handles media inquiries and coordinates press releases and statements from the mayor's office.
NYC Media
Operates NYC TV and other city-owned media channels.
Office of Film, Theater & Broadcasting
Issues permits for film and TV production in the city.
Office of Ethnic & Community Media
Coordinates outreach to ethnic and community media organizations across the city.

Deputy Mayor for Intergovernmental Affairs

▼

Manages the city's relationships with state and federal government, elected officials, and community stakeholders.

Office of Emerging Markets
Builds relationships with underrepresented communities and emerging business sectors.
Community Affairs Unit
The mayor's liaison to community groups, elected officials, and borough-level stakeholders.
Office of Engagement
Coordinates outreach and engagement initiatives on behalf of the mayor's office.
Public Engagement Unit
Proactive outreach unit connecting New Yorkers to city services — housing, benefits, health care.

Structure based on the NYC Office of the Mayor Organization Chart, July 2025, published by the NYC Comptroller's office. CB6-specific context reflects the district's work across agencies.
