Community boards are New York City’s 59 local advisory bodies. Advisory, obviously, means the city does not have to listen. But Hamilton’s standard was that government should be adapted to the real state of the country. Same idea here, just scaled to New York City, Brooklyn, and Community Board 6.

That is the point of this dashboard: real data, real decisions, real neighborhoods. Those choices show up in what we actually review — land use, budget, transportation, landmarks, and more. The East River is not a choice. The Brooklyn Bridge is. Community Board 6 is.
