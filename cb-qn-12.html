Furman Center Neighborhood Profile

Jamaica/Hollis - QN12

View full profile ↗

Summary

In 2023, Jamaica/Hollis was the city’s largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 28th highest median income, and the 36th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 6,226 new housing units, 2,287 units of which were market rate and 3,939 units of which were income restricted.

  • In 2023, Jamaica/Hollis was the city’s largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods.
  • It has the largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 28th highest median income, and the 36th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods.
  • From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 6,226 new housing units, 2,287 units of which were market rate and 3,939 units of which were income restricted.
  • Median household income in 2023 was $78,090, about 2% less than citywide median household income ($79,480).
  • The poverty rate in Jamaica/Hollis was 13.4% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide.
  • Real median gross rent in Jamaica/Hollis increased from $1,410 in 2006 to $1,680 in 2023. This represents a 19.1% increase over the same period.
  • The overall rental vacancy rate in Jamaica/Hollis was 3.5% in 2023.
  • As of 2023, the change in median gross rent outpaced the change in median household income by 16 percentage points.
  • In 2023, 31.5% of renter households in Jamaica/Hollis were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent).
  • 84.0% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 17 percentage points higher than the share in 2010.
  • In 2023, the homeownership rate in Jamaica/Hollis was 46.9%, which is higher than the citywide share of 32.5%. The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has increased by 0.6 percentage points since 2010.
  • Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in Jamaica/Hollis, 5.7% and 6.1% were high cost loans, respectively.
  • 461 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in Jamaica/Hollis in 2022. There were 12.6 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.
  • Among all residential properties, prices of residential properties have increased 92% in Jamaica/Hollis since 2009.
  • Between 2010 and 2024, 6,226 units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Jamaica/Hollis. 37% were market rate, compared to 38% that were income-targeted.
  • From 2010 and 2024, an estimated 38% units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Jamaica/Hollis for low-income households (<=80% AMI).
  • The Department of Buildings issued new building permits to 155 residential units in new buildings in Jamaica/Hollis in 2024, 217 fewer than the number of units authorized in 2023.
  • The Department of Buildings issued new certificates of occupancy to 1,428 residential units in new buildings in Jamaica/Hollis in 2024, 936 more than the number of units certified in 2023.
  • 4.5% of the rental units were public housing rental units, as of 2024.
  • Jamaica/Hollis has 116 properties with subsidized units as of 2024 through programs like HUD assistance, Low Income Housing Tax Credits, 421a, and others.
  • 7 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2025 and 2030, 5 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2031 and 2040, and 19 properties have subsidies expiring after 2040.
  • The serious crime rate, including property and violent crime types, was 13.3 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, compared to 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide.
