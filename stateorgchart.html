Statewide Elected

Governor — Kathy Hochul

Chief executive of New York State. Appoints agency commissioners and proposes the state budget. First female Governor of New York, took office August 2021. Running for a second full term in 2026. governor.ny.gov ↗

Statewide Elected

Lieutenant Governor — Antonio Delgado

Presides over the State Senate, succeeds the Governor if the office becomes vacant. Appointed 2022, elected to full term November 2022. governor.ny.gov ↗

Statewide Elected

Attorney General — Letitia James

Independent legal officer of the state. Represents New York in legal matters, investigates fraud, enforces consumer protection and civil rights laws. ag.ny.gov ↗

Statewide Elected

Comptroller — Thomas DiNapoli

Independent financial watchdog. Audits state agencies, manages the state pension fund, reviews state contracts. osc.ny.gov ↗

Housing & Community Renewal

▼

The state's primary housing agencies — most directly relevant to CB6's work on affordable housing, Gowanus rezoning implementation, and tenant protections.

HCR — Homes & Community Renewal
hcr.ny.gov ↗ · Finances and regulates affordable housing statewide. Administers LIHTC, HOME, and other programs that fund projects in CB6.
HFA — Housing Finance Agency
hcr.ny.gov ↗ · Issues tax-exempt bonds to finance affordable housing developments across New York State.
SONYMA — Mortgage Agency
hcr.ny.gov/sonyma ↗ · Provides low-interest mortgages to first-time homebuyers across New York State.
DHCR — Housing & Community Renewal
hcr.ny.gov ↗ · Administers rent regulation programs including rent stabilization and rent control for NYC tenants.

Transportation & Infrastructure

▼

State agencies overseeing transportation infrastructure, bridges, and transit — directly relevant to CB6's street safety and transit work.

DOT — Dept of Transportation
dot.ny.gov ↗ · Manages state highways, bridges, and coordinates with NYC DOT. Oversees MTA capital funding and Sammy's Law implementation at state level.
MTA — Metropolitan Transportation Authority
mta.info ↗ · State-created public authority operating NYC subway, buses, LIRR, Metro-North, and bridges/tunnels. CB6 is served by multiple subway lines.
Thruway Authority
thruway.ny.gov ↗ · Operates and maintains the New York State Thruway system.
DMV — Dept of Motor Vehicles
dmv.ny.gov ↗ · Licenses drivers, registers vehicles, and administers traffic safety programs statewide.

Health & Human Services

▼

Agencies providing direct services to New Yorkers — healthcare, income support, child welfare, and mental health.

DOH — Dept of Health
health.ny.gov ↗ · Regulates hospitals and health facilities, administers Medicaid, oversees public health programs across the state.
OTDA — Office of Temporary & Disability Assistance
otda.ny.gov ↗ · Administers SNAP, cash assistance, and homeless services programs.
OCFS — Office of Children & Family Services
ocfs.ny.gov ↗ · Oversees child welfare, foster care, juvenile justice, and daycare programs.
OMH — Office of Mental Health
omh.ny.gov ↗ · Operates state psychiatric centers and licenses community mental health programs.
OASAS — Addiction Services
oasas.ny.gov ↗ · Regulates and funds addiction treatment and prevention services across New York.

Environment & Energy

▼

State agencies managing environmental protection, climate, and energy — highly relevant to CB6's Gowanus Canal Superfund work and climate resilience.

DEC — Dept of Environmental Conservation
dec.ny.gov ↗ · Regulates air, water, and land quality. Oversees Superfund cleanup — including the Gowanus Canal — and statewide environmental permitting.
NYSERDA — Energy R&D Authority
nyserda.ny.gov ↗ · Advances clean energy technology, administers energy efficiency programs, and funds offshore wind development.
PSC — Public Service Commission
dps.ny.gov ↗ · Regulates electric, gas, water, and telecom utilities. Sets Con Edison rates that affect CB6 residents and businesses.

Economic Development & Labor

▼

Agencies supporting businesses, workers, and economic growth across New York State.

ESD — Empire State Development
esd.ny.gov ↗ · State's primary economic development agency. Provides business incentives, manages the Brooklyn Marine Terminal redevelopment at the state level.
DOL — Dept of Labor
dol.ny.gov ↗ · Enforces state labor laws, administers unemployment insurance, and oversees workplace safety.
DFS — Dept of Financial Services
dfs.ny.gov ↗ · Regulates banks, insurance companies, and mortgage lenders operating in New York State.
DOS — Dept of State
dos.ny.gov ↗ · Provides local government support, licenses professionals, oversees community development programs, and administers the SLA.
SLA — State Liquor Authority
sla.ny.gov ↗ · Issues and regulates all liquor licenses in New York State. CB6 reviews applications and submits recommendations to the SLA.
Tax & Finance
tax.ny.gov ↗ · Collects state taxes and administers tax programs including the property tax cap that affects NYC budgeting.

Public Safety

▼

State agencies responsible for law enforcement, corrections, and emergency management.

NYSP — State Police
nysp.ny.gov ↗ · Statewide law enforcement agency. Patrols state highways and assists local agencies.
DOCCS — Corrections & Community Supervision
doccs.ny.gov ↗ · Operates state prisons and supervises people on parole.
DHSES — Homeland Security & Emergency Services
dhses.ny.gov ↗ · Coordinates emergency preparedness and response, including for natural disasters and infrastructure failures.
DCJS — Criminal Justice Services
criminaljustice.ny.gov ↗ · Collects crime statistics, manages criminal records, and coordinates criminal justice policy.

Education

▼

The State Education Department is unique — its commissioner is appointed by the Board of Regents, not the Governor.

NYSED — State Education Dept
nysed.gov ↗ · Sets education policy, oversees school districts, and grants professional licenses. The Commissioner is appointed by the Board of Regents.
SUNY — State University of NY
suny.edu ↗ · 64-campus public university system. Chancellor appointed by a Board of Trustees.
CUNY — City University of NY
cuny.edu ↗ · 25-campus public university system serving New York City. Chancellor appointed by a Board of Trustees.

NY State Legislature

The Legislature is bicameral — 63-member Senate and 150-member Assembly. Both houses pass state laws, approve the state budget, and confirm the Governor's appointments.
