This is the district page for Brooklyn Community Board 6.

CB6 covers Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Columbia Street Waterfront, Gowanus, and Park Slope. If you have thoughts on where those lines should actually be drawn — and plenty of people do — feel free to reach out at Mike@bkcb6.org. District Overview — Furman Center
Income & Poverty

The largest household income bracket was $100,001 – $250,000, covering 36.2% of households. The same bracket led in 2000, at 35.5%.

Median household income was $165,910 in 2023—109% above the citywide median of $79,480. The poverty rate was 10.5%, versus 18.2% citywide.

Rents

Real median gross rent rose from $2,020 in 2006 to $2,780 in 2023.

Between 2019-2023, the largest share of studios had gross rents between $2,500.00 – $3,000.00 (20%), the largest share of one-bedrooms had rents between $3,000.00 – $4,000.00 (22%), the largest share of two-bedrooms had rents between $3,000.00 – $4,000.00 (26%), the largest share of three bedrooms had rents > $4k (30%).

Rent growth outpaced income growth by 1.7 percentage points as of 2023. In 2023, 14.4% of renter households in BK CB6 were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent). 17.7% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 8 percentage points higher than the share in 2010.

Homeownership & Sales

In 2023, the homeownership rate in BK CB6 was 39.7%, which is higher than the citywide share of 32.5%. That’s increased 3.7 percentage points since 2010. In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 21.2 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 3.0 per 1,000 properties in the district. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in BK CB6, 1.1% and 1.6% were high cost loans, respectively. 53 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in BK CB6 in 2022. There were 3.2 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.

Among all residential properties, prices of residential properties have increased 119% in BK CB6 since 2009.

Housing Production

Between 2010 and 2024, 3,961 units in 4+ unit buildings were built—81% market rate, 10% income-targeted.

An estimated 10% of those units targeted low-income households at or below 80% AMI.

The Department of Buildings issued new building permits to 628 residential units in new buildings in BK CB6 in 2024, 322 more than the number of units authorized in 2023.

The Department of Buildings issued new certificates of occupancy to 438 residential units in new buildings in BK CB6 in 2024, 214 more than the number of units certified in 2023.

10.4% of rental units were public housing as of 2024.

Crime

The serious crime rate was 10.2 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, versus 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide.

Demographics

The district had an estimated 123,309 residents in 2023, of whom 9.4% identified as Asian, 7.4% identified as Black, 14.8% identified as Hispanic, and 59.9% identified as White.

BK CB6 ranks 43rd in population among NYC’s 59 community board districts. Among the 59 districts, it has the 49th largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 3rd highest median income, and the 9th most expensive rents. From 2010 to 2024, the district added 3,961 new housing units, 3,213 units of which were market rate and 748 units of which were income restricted.
