Park Slope/Carroll Gardens - Brooklyn Community Board Profile

View full profile ↗

Summary

In 2023, Park Slope/Carroll Gardens was the city’s 43rd largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the 49th largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 3rd highest median income, and the 9th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 3,961 new housing units, 3,213 units of which were market rate and 748 units of which were income restricted.

In 2023, there were an estimated 123,309 people in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens, of which 9.4% identified as Asian, 7.4% identified as Black, 14.8% identified as Hispanic, and 59.9% identified as White.

In 2023, the household income group with the largest share (36.2%) of households was $100,001 – $250,000. In 2000, the same household income group had the largest share as well, with a slightly lower share of 35.5%.

Median household income in 2023 was $165,910, about 109% more than citywide median household income ($79,480). The poverty rate in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens was 10.5% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide.

Real median gross rent in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens increased from $2,020 in 2006 to $2,780 in 2023.

Between 2019-2023, the largest share of studios had gross rents between $2,500.00 – $3,000.00 (20%), the largest share of one-bedrooms had rents between $3,000.00 – $4,000.00 (22%), the largest share of two-bedrooms had rents between $3,000.00 – $4,000.00 (26%), the largest share of three bedrooms had rents > $4k (30%). (If you are viewing this page on a desktop, you can use the drop down menu below to switch between different apartment sizes)

As of 2023, the change in median gross rent outpaced the change in median household income by 1.7 percentage points. In 2023, 14.4% of renter households in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent). 17.7% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 8 percentage points higher than the share in 2010.

In 2023, the homeownership rate in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens was 39.7%, which is higher than the citywide share of 32.5%. The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has increased by 3.7 percentage points since 2010. In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 21.2 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 3.0 per 1,000 properties in the neighborhood. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens, 1.1% and 1.6% were high cost loans, respectively. 53 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens in 2022. There were 3.2 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.

Among all residential properties, prices of residential properties have increased 119% in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens since 2009. Price appreciation by property type is only available if there are a minimum of 10 sales recorded in any given year for the specified period. (If you are viewing this page on a desktop, you can use the drop down menu below to switch to a different index year for longer term trends.)

Between 2010 and 2024, 3,961 units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens. 81% were market rate, compared to 10% that were income-targeted. We use data from a variety of sources to count the number of income-restricted units targeted to households earning between 80% and 165% of AMI. However, due to restricted availability of granular data our calculations of income-restricted units should be read as conservative estimates.

From 2010 and 2024, an estimated 10% units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens for low-income households (<=80% AMI).

The Department of Buildings issued new building permits to 628 residential units in new buildings in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens in 2024, 322 more than the number of units authorized in 2023.

The Department of Buildings issued new certificates of occupancy to 438 residential units in new buildings in Park Slope/Carroll Gardens in 2024, 214 more than the number of units certified in 2023.

10.4% of the rental units were public housing rental units, as of 2024.

The serious crime rate, including property and violent crime types, was 10.2 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, compared to 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide.
