Fordham/University Heights Community Profile

View full profile ↗

Summary

In 2023, Fordham/University Heights was the city’s 38th largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. It has the 6th largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 51st highest median income, and the 46th most expensive rents of the city’s 59 neighborhoods. From 2010 to 2024, the neighborhood added 4,152 new housing units, 1,394 units of which were market rate and 2,758 units of which were income restricted.

In 2023, there were an estimated 128,490 people in Fordham/University Heights, of which 5.3% identified as Asian, 13.1% identified as Black, 76.9% identified as Hispanic, and 3.0% identified as White.

In 2023, the household income group with the largest share (25.8%) of households was <= $20,000. In 2000, the same household income group had the largest share as well, with a slightly higher share of 31.7%.

Real median gross rent in Fordham/University Heights increased from $1,250 in 2006 to $1,480 in 2023. This represents a 18.4% increase over the same period. The overall rental vacancy rate in Fordham/University Heights was 2.9% in 2023.

Between 2019-2023, the largest share of studios had gross rents between $1,000.00 – $1,500.00 (42%), the largest share of one-bedrooms had rents between $1,500.00 – $2,000.00 (42%), the largest share of two-bedrooms had rents between $1,500.00 – $2,000.00 (40%), the largest share of three bedrooms had rents between $1,500.00 – $2,000.00 (26%).

As of 2023, the change in median household income outpaced the change in median gross rent by 23.7 percentage points. In 2023, 37.7% of renter households in Fordham/University Heights were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent). 97.5% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 16 percentage points higher than the share in 2010.

In 2023, the homeownership rate in Fordham/University Heights was 8.9%, which is lower than the citywide share of 32.5%. The homeownership rate in the neighborhood has increased by 5.8 percentage points since 2010. In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 28.4 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 6.2 per 1,000 properties in the neighborhood. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in Fordham/University Heights, 6.0% and 22.2% were high cost loans, respectively. 27 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in Fordham/University Heights in 2022. There were 14.8 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units.

Among all residential properties, prices of residential properties have increased 90% in Fordham/University Heights since 2009.

Between 2010 and 2024, 4,152 units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Fordham/University Heights. 34% were market rate, compared to 51% that were income-targeted. From 2010 and 2024, an estimated 51% units in 4+ unit buildings were built in Fordham/University Heights for low-income households (<=80% AMI).

The Department of Buildings issued new building permits to 699 residential units in new buildings in Fordham/University Heights in 2024, 78 fewer than the number of units authorized in 2023.

The Department of Buildings issued new certificates of occupancy to 1,169 residential units in new buildings in Fordham/University Heights in 2024, 386 more than the number of units certified in 2023.

2.8% of the rental units were public housing rental units, as of 2024.

Fordham/University Heights has 472 properties with subsidized units as of 2024 through programs like HUD assistance, Low Income Housing Tax Credits, 421a, and others. 18 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2025 and 2030, 46 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2031 and 2040, and 51 properties have subsidies expiring after 2040.

The serious crime rate, including property and violent crime types, was 18.9 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, compared to 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide.
