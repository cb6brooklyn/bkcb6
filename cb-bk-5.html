District Overview — Furman Center

In 2023, East New York/Starrett City was the city’s 10th largest neighborhood by population out of 59 neighborhoods. Among the 59 districts, it has the 7th largest proportion of nonwhite population, the 47th highest median income, and the 47th most expensive rents. From 2010 to 2024, the district added 7,263 new housing units, 1,191 units of which were market rate and 6,065 units of which were income restricted. In 2023, there were an estimated 190,021 people in East New York/Starrett City, of which 8.8% identified as Asian, 45.0% identified as Black, 35.8% identified as Hispanic, and 3.5% identified as White. In 2023, the household income group with the largest share (23.0%) of households was $60,001 – $100,000. In 2000, the household income group with the largest share (24.4%) of households was <= $20,000. Median household income in 2023 was $51,220, about 36% less than citywide median household income ($79,480). The poverty rate in East New York/Starrett City was 22.0% in 2023 compared to 18.2% citywide. Real median gross rent in East New York/Starrett City increased from $1,310 in 2006 to $1,410 in 2023. This represents a 7.6% increase over the same period. The overall rental vacancy rate in East New York/Starrett City was 3.4% in 2023. As of 2023, the change in median household income outpaced the change in median gross rent by 31.3 percentage points. In 2023, 29.7% of renter households in East New York/Starrett City were severely rent burdened (spent more than 50% of household income on rent). 90.5% of the rental units were affordable at 80% Area Median Income, 9 percentage points higher than the share in 2010. In 2023, the homeownership rate in East New York/Starrett City was 21.3%, which is lower than the citywide share of 32.5%. The homeownership rate in the district has decreased by 1.8 percentage points since 2010. In 2023, the home purchase loan rate was 16.1 per 1,000 properties (owner-occupied 1-4 family buildings, condominiums, or cooperative apartments) and the refinance loan rate was 6.2 per 1,000 properties in the district. Out of all the first-time home purchase loans and refinance loans in East New York/Starrett City, 5.4% and 5.4% were high cost loans, respectively. 302 properties had a filing of mortgage foreclosure in East New York/Starrett City in 2022. There were 16.6 mortgage foreclosure actions initiated per 1,000 1-4 family properties and condominium units. Among all residential properties, prices of residential properties have increased 89% in East New York/Starrett City since 2009. Between 2010 and 2024, 7,263 units in 4+ unit buildings were built in East New York/Starrett City. 16% were market rate, compared to 81% that were income-targeted. From 2010 and 2024, an estimated 81% units in 4+ unit buildings were built in East New York/Starrett City for low-income households (<=80% AMI). The Department of Buildings issued new building permits to 2,036 residential units in new buildings in East New York/Starrett City in 2024, 1,696 more than the number of units authorized in 2023. The Department of Buildings issued new certificates of occupancy to 1,821 residential units in new buildings in East New York/Starrett City in 2024, 1,027 more than the number of units certified in 2023. 8.8% of the rental units were public housing rental units, as of 2024. East New York/Starrett City has 733 properties with subsidized units as of 2024 through programs like HUD assistance, Low Income Housing Tax Credits, 421a, and others. 19 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2025 and 2030, 35 properties have subsidies due to expire between 2031 and 2040, and 128 properties have subsidies expiring after 2040. The serious crime rate, including property and violent crime types, was 17.0 serious crimes per 1,000 residents in 2024, compared to 13.6 serious crimes per 1,000 residents citywide.
